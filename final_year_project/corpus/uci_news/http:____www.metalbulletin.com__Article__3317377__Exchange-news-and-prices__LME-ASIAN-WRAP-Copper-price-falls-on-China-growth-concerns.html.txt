Content :London Metal Exchange copper traded in a range on Monday March 10 on increasing China growth worries after the country's first ever corporate bond default. At 04:20 London time, three-month copper stood at $6,680 per tonne, down $16 from its opening price. The contract fell to a low of $6,608 during the morning session. “There was some chart-support as prices entered into oversold area, but the hangover hasn’t retreated after China’s government refused to bail out Chaori,” a Shanghai- based analyst said.
